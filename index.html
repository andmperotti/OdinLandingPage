Wildlife in San Francisco

During a may game while the Giants were battling the Cincinnati Reds, a pelican flew into the ballpark and landed on the outfield grass, after a while it flew off leaving everyone mesmerized.

Sea Lions started appearing on the piers in San Francisco in 1989 after the Loma Prieta earthquake, and have since hit population highs of 300 in pier 39.

Great Blue Heron's are very common to see around San Francisco's many bodies of water.

Red-tailed Hawks are commonly seen in the presidio area year-round, as that area has many trees and forested areas.
